The Session is a psychological slasher film project built in Unity, focused on mood, pacing, and interactive scene presentation. I worked on shaping the experience through visual framing, narrative flow, and a darker atmosphere that supports the story.

Introduction

The Session explores suspense through a Unity-built slasher experience, using controlled scenes, cinematic framing, and narrative choices to create a darker, story-driven project.

Lessons Learned

01

Strong framing shapes emotional payoff

Building tension worked best when the camera, composition, and interface all supported the same emotional beat. Small visual decisions had a huge impact on how serious a scene felt.

02

Player choices need clear context

Choices become more meaningful when the player understands the tone, the stakes, and the consequence of what they are about to do. Clarity makes tension more effective, not less.

03

Constraints made the narrative sharper

Working within a focused set of scenes and interactions helped the project feel more intentional. Limiting scope made it easier to refine mood, pacing, and story delivery.
